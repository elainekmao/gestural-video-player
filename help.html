GestureVid A video player you control with your mouse movements!

Need help with GestureVid?

Thank you for choosing GestureVid! Whether it's your first time using GestureVid, or you just need to refresh your memory, this page will help you learn the basics of using this unique, gesture-controlled video player.


Basic Controls
Playing and pausing the video

If the video is paused, use your mouse to draw the letter "P" (using only one stroke) anywhere in the browser window, and the video will start playing! If the video is already playing, you can pause it by drawing another "P".

Volume controls

To increase the volume of the video, use your mouse to draw an upward "^" arrow shape. To decrease the volume, draw a downward arrow or "v" shape.

To mute the video, use your mouse to draw a letter "M". If the video is already muted, draw another "M" to unmute it.

Advanced controls
Skipping forward or backward

To skip forward in the video, use your mouse to draw an arrow pointing toward the right. This will skip you forward by 5 seconds. To skip backward, use your mouse to draw an arrow pointing to the left. This will skip you backward by 5 seconds.

Changing playback speed

To make the video play at a faster speed, use your mouse to draw a curlicue from left to right. To slow down the video, draw a curlicue from right to left.
